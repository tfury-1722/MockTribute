Robert Kiyosaki

Robert Kiyosaki's a legendary figure who has changed the way how people look at money. An entrepreneur, investor, author and motivational speaker by profession, he audaciously is said to have commented that the reason why most of the people today are struggling financially is because despite years of formal education and training, they do not know anything about money. He is the author of the book, 'Rich Dad Poor Dad', which ever since the time of its release, scaled to become the number one personal finance book of all time. The book draws a comparison between his biological dad who was educated but poor and fictitious dad who was a college dropout but the wealthiest man in Hawaii.



Robert Kiyosaki

TimeLine

  • 8 April 1947 Kiyosaki was born in Hilo, in the American Territory of Hawaii, into a family of Japanese descent.

  • 1969 - 1974 He enrolled at the U.S. Merchant Marine Academy in New York, graduating from the same as a deck officer.

  • 1972 He served during the Vietnam War as a helicopter gunship pilot in the Marine Corps. For his service, he was awarded with the Air Medal.

  • 1974 He left the Marine Corps. However, instead of returning to Hawaii he moved to New York. He also started attending Erhard EST seminars.

  • 1977 He started a company of his own that brought forth to the market the first nylon and Velcro "surfer" wallets. The company was named "Rippers" but eventually went bankrupt.

  • 1974-1978 He was a salesman for Xerox Corporation selling copy machines.

  • 1979 He began working as a motivational speaker for a personal growth seminar business called Money and You, along with D.C Cordova.

  • 1980 He initiated a business that licensed T-shirts for heavy metal rock bands, such as Motley Crue.

  • 1985 With a change in trend, the preference for heavy metal band gave way to softer music which led to a decline in demand and the company became insolvent.

  • 1986 - 2017 He tied the knot with Kim Meyer, who is an entrepreneur, investor, author, and motivational speaker.

  • 1 April 1993 He published his first book, "If You Want to Be Rich and Happy, Don't Go To School". In his book, he encouraged parents not to send their children to college and instead to enter the real estate business.

  • 1994 He took up early retirement. He was only 47 years of age then.

  • 2000 "Rich Dad Poor Dad" is published advocating financial independence and teaching its readers the importance of building wealth through investments in real estate and the starting and owning of business.

  • 2000 "Cashflow Quadrant: Rich Dad's Guide to Financial Freedom" and "Rich Dad's Guide to Investing: What the Rich Invest in, That the Poor and the Middle Class Do Not!" were published.
